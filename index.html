Stray Animals in San Antonio

As someone who was born and raised in San Antonio I have noticed a overwhelming amount of stray animals wondering the streets. According to the asilomar reports from the Animal Care Services of San Antonio in 2023 there was a reported 2,594 healthy cats and dogs that were euthenized due to over capacity in the shelter. That is over a 100% increse from 2022 where there was a reported 1,055 euthenized cats and dogs. As a advid animal lover this breaks my heart. As a community we need to come together and do better.

Solving The Problem

Lowering euthanization rates and keeping animals off the streets of San Antonio does not have one simple solution.

Pet Adoption

An increase in pet adoptions will create space for more aniamls and gives them a chance to find forever homes.

Neutering

There is many misconceptions that nutering animals is unethical, however this is incorrect. Neutering animals is a key solution in lowering euthanization rates due to less animals being breed.

Donations

Donations are greatly appreciated at all animal shelters. They accept all kinds of donations even pet supplies and unwanted blankets.
