Stray Animals in San Antonio

As someone who was born and raised in San Antonio, I have noticed an overwhelming number of stray animals wondering the streets. According to the Asilomar reports from the Animal Care Services of San Antonio in 2023 there was a reported 2,594 healthy cats and dogs that were euthanized due to overcapacity in the shelter. That is over a 100% increase from 2022 where there was a reported 1,055 euthanized cats and dogs. As an avid animal lover this breaks my heart. As a community we need to come together and do better.

Solving The Problem

Lowering euthanasia rates and keeping animals off the streets of San Antonio does not have one simple solution.

Pet Adoption

An increase in pet adoptions will create space for more animals and gives them a chance to find forever homes.

Neutering

There are many misconceptions that neutering animals is unethical, however this is incorrect. Neutering animals is a key solution in lowering euthanasia rates due to fewer animals being bred.

Donations

Donations are appreciated at all animal shelters. They accept all kinds of donations, even pet supplies and unwanted blankets.
